Sea Walk

Sea walking is a unique and exciting activity that can be experienced in the Andaman Islands. This activity allows visitors to walk on the sea bed and observe the marine life up close and personal. During a sea walk, visitors are fitted with a helmet that provides a supply of air, allowing them to breathe normally while walking on the sea floor. This is a great opportunity to see the vibrant coral reefs, schools of colorful fish, and other marine life in their natural habitat. The experience is suitable for people of all ages and requires no prior diving or swimming experience. The guides accompanying the walk provide a detailed briefing and are always on hand to assist. Overall, a sea walk in the Andaman Islands is a truly unique and unforgettable experience, offering a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to explore the underwater world and appreciate the beauty of the island's marine life. Whether you are a seasoned diver or just looking for a fun and unusual activity, sea walking is a must-try experience in the Andaman Islands.

Scuba Diving

Scuba diving in the Andaman Islands is a truly unforgettable experience. The islands are home to a rich and diverse marine environment, with crystal clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and an abundance of marine life, including colorful fish, sea turtles, and dolphins. Scuba diving in the Andaman Islands is suitable for people of all levels, from beginners to experienced divers. The diving schools and instructors offer a range of diving courses, including Open Water and Advanced Open Water, as well as guided dives and snorkeling trips. Dives are usually done from a dive boat, with the dive sites located around the islands, including the Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park and the Ross and Smith Islands. Dives are typically around 40 to 50 feet deep, offering ample time to explore the underwater environment and admire the beauty of the coral reefs and marine life. Overall, scuba diving in the Andaman Islands is a must-try experience for those looking to explore the underwater world and discover the beauty of the island's marine environment. Whether you are a seasoned diver or just starting out, the Andaman Islands offer a truly unique and unforgettable diving experience.

Radha-Nagar

Radha Nagar Beach, located in Havelock Island, Andaman & Nicobar Islands, is considered to be one of the most beautiful and picturesque beaches in India. The beach is named after Radha, a character from Hindu mythology. The history of Radha Nagar Beach dates back to the early 20th century, when the British established a naval base on Havelock Island. During World War II, the beach served as a landing spot for British troops and supplies. Following the war, the beach was largely untouched and remained a hidden gem until the 1990s, when the Indian government began promoting tourism in the Andaman & Nicobar Islands. In 2004, Time magazine named Radha Nagar Beach as Asia's Best Beach, which brought it to the world's attention and made it one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Andaman & Nicobar Islands. Today, Radha Nagar Beach is known for its crystal-clear waters, white sand, and lush green surroundings, and is a must-visit destination for travelers to Havelock Island. Whether you want to relax on the beach, swim in the sea, or engage in various water sports, Radha Nagar Beach has something to offer for everyone.

Today, Chidiya Tapu is a popular destination for visitors to the Andaman & Nicobar Islands, offering stunning views of the Bay of Bengal and a unique and diverse birdlife.
