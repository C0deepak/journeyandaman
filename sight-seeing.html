Natural Rock - Neil

The Natural Bridge, also known as Howrah Bridge, is a unique natural formation located in Neil Island, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, India. The bridge is formed by the erosion of limestone rock by the sea and is a popular tourist attraction. The bridge connects two parts of the island and is a remarkable sight, with the turquoise waters of the sea rushing beneath it. The bridge is surrounded by lush tropical vegetation and is a great spot for snorkeling and swimming. Visitors can walk on the bridge and enjoy the stunning views of the sea and the surrounding landscape. The Natural Bridge is a testament to the power of nature and is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Neil Island.

Limestone Caves Baratang

The Limestone Caves of Baratang are a network of caves located in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands of India. These caves are formed due to the erosion of limestone rock by the sea and are a popular tourist destination. They are known for their stunning stalactite and stalagmite formations, which are formed by the dripping of mineral-rich water from the roof of the caves over thousands of years. Visitors can take a ferry ride through the mangrove forests to reach the caves. The caves are also home to a variety of rare and exotic species of birds and bats. The caves are well-maintained and provide a unique opportunity to experience the natural beauty of the region.

NorthBay ~ Ross_Island

North Bay and Ross Islands are two of the most popular tourist destinations in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, India. North Bay is known for its stunning coral reefs and turquoise waters, making it a popular spot for snorkeling and scuba diving. The island is home to a variety of exotic marine life, including colorful fish, sea turtles, and coral formations. Ross Island, on the other hand, was once the administrative headquarters of the British during their rule in India. The island is now abandoned and is a popular spot for sightseeing and exploring the remnants of colonial-era structures, including a church, a hospital, and a museum. Visitors can take a ferry ride to reach the islands and enjoy the natural beauty and historical significance of the region.

Elephanta ~ Radha Nagar

Elephanta and Radhanagar Beach are two must-visit destinations for anyone traveling to Havelock, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, India. Elephanta is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is known for its rock-cut cave temples that date back to the 7th century. The caves are filled with stunning sculptures and carvings that showcase the rich cultural and religious heritage of the region. Radhanagar Beach, on the other hand, is widely considered as one of the best beaches in Asia and is known for its pristine waters and white sand. The beach is surrounded by lush tropical vegetation and is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing. Visitors can take a ferry ride or a scenic drive to reach both destinations and enjoy the natural beauty and cultural richness of the region.

Cellular Jail

The Cellular Jail, also known as Kala Pani, is a historical monument located in Port Blair, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, India. The jail was built by the British in the late 19th century and was used to imprison political prisoners during the independence movement. The jail is known for its unique architectural design, with its seven wings radiating from a central tower, making it impossible for prisoners to communicate with each other. Visitors can take a guided tour of the jail and learn about its history and the struggles of Indian independence activists who were imprisoned there. The jail also houses a museum that showcases the artifacts and photographs from the colonial era, providing a glimpse into the past. The Cellular Jail is a sobering reminder of India's struggle for freedom and is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history and culture.

Sunset Point - Chidiyatapu

Chidiya Tapu Beach is a beautiful beach located in South Andaman, India, and is known for its stunning sunsets. The beach is surrounded by lush tropical vegetation and is a popular spot for birdwatching, with a variety of exotic bird species found in the area. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the sea and the surrounding landscape while relaxing on the beach or taking a leisurely walk. The sunset at Chidiya Tapu Beach is a breathtaking spectacle, with the sun sinking into the sea, painting the sky with brilliant shades of orange and red. Visitors can enjoy the sunset from the beach or from one of the nearby hilltop viewpoints, providing panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Chidiya Tapu Beach is a must-visit destination for anyone looking for a peaceful and serene getaway.
